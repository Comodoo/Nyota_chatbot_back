STRIKE AND INDUSTRIAL ACTION PROMPTS - PART VIII
[Strike] What are the requirements for a lawful strike?
According to Section 63 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, right to strike may only be exercised if: (a) dispute is of interest not right; (b) issue referred to Court and dispute resolution exhausted; (i) order granted that dispute remains unsolved; (ii) 30 days expired since order; (c) at least 48 hours prior written notice given to employer; (d) in case of Government employer, at least 7 days notice. Any strike conducted accordingly is lawful.

[Strike] What are the limitations on right to strike?
According to Section 64 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, no person shall take part in strike if: (a) employee of public authority engaged in management; (b) employee engaged in management of employer's business; (c) employee in essential service or minimum service. Also cannot strike if: (a) dispute relates to current collective bargaining agreement; (b) state of emergency officially declared.

[Strike] What are essential services where strikes are prohibited?
According to Section 64(2) of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, essential services are: (a) water and sanitation; (b) electricity; (c) health services and associated services; (d) air traffic control and civil aviation telecommunication; (e) fire services; (f) transport services necessary for operation of foregoing services.

[Strike] What protection exists for lawful strikers?
According to Section 66 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, employee participating in lawful strike shall not be deemed to have breached employment contract and no civil or criminal liability lies against employee. Employer not obliged to remunerate employee for services not rendered during strike. If remuneration in kind for basic amenities, employer shall not discontinue but may recover monetary value.

[Strike] What are the requirements for strike ballots?
According to Section 62 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, right to strike exercised upon proper secret ballot called according to trade union rules. In absence of rules, ballot conducted in transparent and fair manner. Any member may apply to Court to nullify ballot on grounds of fraud, non-observance of rules or unfairness. Court may give restraining order pending determination.

[Strike] What are the rules for secondary strikes?
According to Section 67 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, no person shall take part in secondary strike unless: (a) strike to be supported is lawful; (b) employer received written notice at least 7 days prior; (c) secondary strike reasonable in justification and impact. Court may grant injunction restraining secondary strike if contravenes Act or unjustified.

[Strike] What are the rules for replacement labour during strikes?
According to Section 68 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, employer shall not engage temporary employment service during strike except as provided in regulation. Employer shall not engage temporary service for work of locked-out employees unless lock-out in response to strike. Minister in consultation with Labour Advisory Board makes regulation for temporary replacement labour for maintenance service.

[Strike] What is lawful picketing?
According to Section 70 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, lawful for registered trade union to authorise picket by members for purpose of peacefully demonstrating: (a) in support of lawful strike; (b) persuading employees not taking part to support strike; (c) opposition to lawful lock-out. Picket may be held: (a) in public place outside employer's premises; (b) with permission inside employer's premises.
