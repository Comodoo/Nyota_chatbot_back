PRACTICAL SCENARIO PROMPTS
[Practical] A trade union with 25 members wants to register in Zanzibar. What steps?
According to Sections 15, 28 and 29 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005: 1. Apply to Registrar within 6 months of formation; 2. Submit: prescribed form, 2 certified constitution copies, other information, prescribed fee; 3. Constitution must include all Schedule matters; 4. Have registered office and postal address; 5. Notify Registrar of office location. Constitution alterations sent to Registrar within 1 month. Registrar issues certificate if requirements met.

[Practical] Employee dismissed for trade union activities. Recourse?
According to Sections 5 and 11 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005: 1. File complaint with Labour Court; 2. Employer must prove dismissal for valid reason (not trade union activities); 3. If Court finds complaint well-founded, may order: reinstatement if requested; restoration of benefits; compensation for damages; punitive indemnity if appropriate. Complaint must show dismissal related to trade union membership or activities.

[Practical] Trade union planning strike. Requirements?
According to Sections 62 and 63 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005: 1. Conduct secret ballot according to union rules or transparent fair manner; 2. Ensure dispute is of interest (not right); 3. Exhaust dispute resolution: refer to Court, get unresolved certificate, wait 30 days; 4. Give 48 hours written notice to employer (7 days if Government); 5. Not in essential service; 6. Not prohibited by current agreement or state of emergency.

[Practical] Employer and union negotiating collective agreement. Process?
According to Sections 57-61 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005: 1. Representative union (majority at bargaining level) has exclusive bargaining right; 2. Parties bargain in good faith with effort to reach agreement; 3. Employer discloses relevant information for effective bargaining; 4. Agreement on: work conditions, employment terms, relations regulation; 5. Submit agreement to Court for registration; 6. Court examines, may register, modify with consent, or refuse if unlawful or below standards.

[Practical] Dispute between employer and employees. Resolution steps?
According to Sections 73-75 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005: 1. File dispute with Dispute Handling Unit; 2. Referred to mediator for 30-day mediation; 3. If settled, written agreement signed; 4. If not settled, certificate of unresolved dispute issued; 5. For essential service: compulsory arbitration within 14 days; 6. For other disputes: arbitration if parties agree or industrial action if lawful; 7. Dispute of right: arbitration for prescribed matters or Court adjudication.

[Practical] Trade union wants to amend constitution. Procedure?
According to Sections 28 and 33 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005: 1. Make alteration according to union's constitution procedures; 2. Send copy of alteration to Registrar within 1 month; 3. Pay prescribed fee for registration; 4. Alteration takes effect from registration date unless later date specified; 5. If change of name: notify Registrar within 1 month, signed by secretary and 2 members; 6. Registrar may refuse name if identical or misleading.

[Practical] Employee wants to authorize union dues deduction. Process?
According to Section 51 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005: 1. Employee authorizes employer in writing to deduct subscriptions from wages; 2. Employer makes deduction as soon as possible; 3. Remits amount to union before next salary payment; 4. Employee may revoke by giving 3 months written notice to employer and union; 5. Employer ceases deduction after 3 months following revocation; 6. Employer shall not charge administrative fees unless agreed in collective agreement.

[Practical] Trade union officer wants workplace access. Rights?
According to Section 50 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005: 1. Office bearer entitled to enter workplace upon reasonable notice to employer; 2. Purpose: recruit members, communicate with members, serve members' interests; 3. Rights subject to reasonable conditions on time, place, manner necessary for safety, property protection, preventing undue work disruption; 4. For domestic sector: no right to enter employer's home without agreement; 5. May conduct lawful meetings and elections at workplace.
