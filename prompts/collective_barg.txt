COLLECTIVE BARGAINING PROMPTS - PART VII
[Collective Bargaining] What is the right to collective bargaining?
According to Section 54 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, right to collective bargaining shall apply to all employees not excluded under this Act. Excluded categories: (a) public officers actually engaged in administration of government affairs; (b) senior employees actually in management of affairs of employee; (c) any other category Minister may exclude by notice in consultation with Labour Advisory Board.

[Collective Bargaining] What are the bargaining subjects?
According to Section 55 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, employer may enter collective agreement with trade unions on: (a) determining work conditions and terms of employment; (b) regulating relations between employers and employees; (c) regulating relations between employers or organisations and trade unions; (d) regulating any matter permitted by employment law; (e) regulating any matter parties deem necessary.

[Collective Bargaining] What are the levels of bargaining?
According to Section 56 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, collective bargaining may take place at: (a) enterprise level; (b) industry or sector level; (c) national level. Employer and trade union shall determine level for each case or generally agree through collective agreement.

[Collective Bargaining] What is a representative trade union?
According to Section 57 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, a representative trade union has exclusive right to bargain with employer. Representative trade union means registered trade union that represents majority of employees at appropriate bargaining level and recognised as such. Trade union seeking recognition shall raise issue with employer. If employer does not recognise or objection raised, matter referred to disputes resolution authority.

[Collective Bargaining] What is the duty to bargain in good faith?
According to Section 59 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, employer and trade unions have duty to bargain in good faith. This includes: (a) make every effort to reach agreement; (b) engage in genuine and constructive negotiation; (c) avoid unjustified delays.

[Collective Bargaining] What information must employers disclose?
According to Section 58 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, where employer is bargaining with representative trade union, employer shall disclose all relevant information allowing effective collective bargaining. Employer not required to disclose: (a) legally privileged information; (b) confidential information causing substantial harm; (c) private personal information without consent. Disputes about information referred to Court.

[Collective Bargaining] How are collective agreements registered?
According to Section 61 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, all collective agreements shall be submitted to Court and copy to Commission for registration. Court keeps register. Court examines agreement and may: (a) register without modification; (b) register with modifications parties consent to; (c) refuse registration if unenforceable or inconsistent with law or minimum standards.

[Collective Bargaining] When are collective agreements binding?
According to Section 60 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, collective agreement between trade union and single employer binds employer and employees who are members of trade union in bargaining unit. Unless otherwise provided, employees not members deemed represented where majority are members. Agreement remains binding for whole period to every person bound.
