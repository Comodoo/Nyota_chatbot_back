HISTORICAL AND CONTEXTUAL PROMPTS
[Historical] When did the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005 come into force?
According to Section 1 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, Act cited as Labour Relations Act 2005 and comes into operation on date Minister appoints by notice in gazette. Minister may defer operation of any part for up to 12 months from commencement. Act assented by President Amani Abeid Karume on 5th March 2005 and passed by House of Representatives on 19th January 2005.

[Historical] What laws did this Act repeal?
According to Section 100 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, repealed Acts: (a) Trade Unions Act, No. 4 of 2001; (b) Industrial Court Act, No. 2 of 1994. Notwithstanding repeal: (a) trade unions registered under repealed Act deemed registered under this Act from original registration date; (b) applications submitted under repealed law dealt with under that law but deemed submitted under this Act; (c) pending matters dealt with as circumstances require.

[Historical] What is the application scope of the Act?
According to Section 2 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, Act applies to: (a) all employees engaged to work in Zanzibar; (b) all employers who engage such employees. Does not apply to: (a) Judges and Judiciary officers; (b) Members of Special Departments; (c) Employees of House of Representatives. Applies to Free Economic Zones with restrictions Minister prescribes in consultation with Labour Advisory Board.

[Historical] How does Act handle international affiliations?
According to Section 10 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, any trade union, employers' organisation or federation may affiliate with and participate in international workers or employers' associations, make financial and other contributions, receive financial and other assistance from them. Allows connection with global labour movements under domestic legal framework.

[Historical] What is the interpretation approach for this Act?
According to Section 3(1) of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, interpretation shall be made in manner giving effect to obligation of Zanzibar under various International Labour treaties to which United Republic of Tanzania as member State of International Labour Organisation has ratified. International labour standards inform interpretation.

[Historical] How are regulations made under this Act?
According to Sections 49 and 99 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, Minister may make Regulations for carrying out provisions of Act. For Part I-VI, regulations cover: prescribed matters, books and registers, registration manner, audit qualifications, inspection conditions, fund disposal, benevolent funds, fees. Minister generally makes regulations for better carrying out of provisions.

[Historical] What transitional provisions exist?
According to Section 100 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, notwithstanding repeal: (a) trade unions registered under repealed Act deemed registered under this Act; (b) applications submitted under repealed law dealt with under that law but deemed submitted under this Act; (c) pending matters dealt with accordingly as circumstances require. Ensures continuity during transition.

[Historical] Who assented to this Act?
According to page 7 of Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, Act assented by Amani Abeid Karume, President of Zanzibar and Chairman of Revolutionary Council on 5th March 2005. Act passed by House of Representatives on 19th January 2005. Clerk of House of Representatives: Khamis Juma Chande.
