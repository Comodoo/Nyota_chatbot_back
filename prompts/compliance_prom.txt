COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT PROMPTS
[Compliance] What are the penalties for offences?
According to Section 98 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, any person who: (a) conducts functions of trade union in name of entity not registered in Zanzibar; (b) recruits members for trade union not registered in Zanzibar; (c) contravenes Sections 5(1) or 37; shall be guilty of offence and liable to fine not less than 400,000 shillings or imprisonment not less than 3 months.

[Compliance] What constitutes contempt of Industrial Court?
According to Section 93 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, any person who: (a) wilfully disobeys Court order; (b) publishes proceedings without authorisation; (c) shows disrespect to proceedings; (d) discloses evidence held in camera; (e) disrupts proceedings; (f) intimidates witness; (g) victimizes person after giving evidence; (h) scandalizes Court; (i) prejudices fair hearing; is guilty of contempt and liable to imprisonment up to 3 months.

[Compliance] How are offences prosecuted?
According to Section 97 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, no prosecution instituted except by, at instance of, or with written consent of Director of Public Prosecutions. All offences tried by Regional Magistrate Court.

[Compliance] What are the financial reporting requirements?
According to Section 45 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, treasurer of registered trade union shall furnish annually to Registrar by March 31: audited statement of receipts and expenditure for 12 months ending December 31, and assets and liabilities as at December 31. Statement accompanied by auditor's report. Treasurer also furnishes membership statement by February 28 showing members on December 31 and state of contributions.

[Compliance] What powers does Registrar have over trade union finances?
According to Section 46 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, Registrar or authorised person upon official complaint or reasonable grounds has access to all financial records at reasonable times, entitled to inspect cash in hand. Every officer shall furnish financial information as required. Opposing, obstructing or impeding Registrar is offence with fine not less than 200,000 shillings or imprisonment up to 3 months.

[Compliance] What are the requirements for trade union accounts?
According to Section 44 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, every treasurer and official responsible for accounts shall, upon resigning or vacating office and at least once year as at December 31, render just and true account of all money received and paid, balance remaining, and all property entrusted. Accounts audited by registered auditor approved by Executive Committee, completed by March 31.

[Compliance] How is legal process served on trade unions?
According to Section 48 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, every summons, notice or document required to be served on trade union in civil or criminal proceedings shall be served on chairman, treasurer, secretary or any official. In default of personal service, sufficient if delivered at registered office or posted to registered postal address.

[Compliance] What are the meeting requirements for trade unions?
According to Section 35 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, every trade union shall in Constitution provide for holding of annual general meeting. Meeting need not involve all members as long as interests fairly represented. Constitution may provide for constituent representation or other fair representation forms.
