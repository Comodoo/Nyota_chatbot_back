REGISTRATION PROMPTS - PART III
[Registration] What are the requirements for trade union registration?
According to Section 15 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, any (a) unregistered trade union composed of twenty (20) or more members; (b) federation of such trade unions or employers' organisation; (c) employers' organisation composed of five or more members; which complies with this Act may apply to the Registrar for registration within six months of its formation. Application must include: (a) prescribed form properly completed; (b) two certified copies of the constitution and rules; (c) any other information assisting the registrar; (d) prescribed fee.

[Registration] What are the grounds for refusal of trade union registration?
According to Section 21 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, the Registrar may refuse to register any trade union or organisation if satisfied that: (a) it has not complied with the provisions of this Act or Regulations; (b) the objects are not in accordance with the definition of trade union; (c) it consists of persons engaged in more than one trade and its constitution does not contain suitable provisions for protection of their respective trade union interests; (d) it is a branch of a trade union other than a registered trade union.

[Registration] What is the certificate of registration?
According to Section 18 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, the Registrar on registering trade union or organisation shall issue a certificate in the prescribed form and that certificate, unless proved to have been cancelled or withdrawn, shall be conclusive evidence for all purposes that the trade union or organisation has been duly registered under this Act.

[Registration] What happens if a trade union is not registered?
According to Section 14 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, every trade union or organisation not registered before the commencement of this Act shall either apply to be registered or be dissolved, within a period of six months from the commencement of this Act or the date of its formation, whichever is the later. Every trade union formed after commencement must apply within six months of formation or be dissolved.

[Registration] What are the consequences of registration cancellation?
According to Section 24 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, if registration is cancelled: (a) the trade union ceases to enjoy rights of registered trade union; (b) the trade union shall be dissolved and funds disposed according to constitution; (c) certificate must be delivered to Registrar within 60 days; (d) no person shall take part in management except for legal proceedings or dissolution.

[Registration] How can trade union registration be cancelled?
According to Section 22 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, registration may be cancelled by Registrar: (a) at request of trade union upon dissolution; (b) if satisfied trade union has ceased to exist. Registrar may apply to Court to cancel registration if satisfied: (a) trade union ceased to be independent of employer; (b) actual objects would have justified refusal of registration; (c) any principal object is unlawful; (d) constitution is unlawful; (e) used for unlawful purpose; (f) contravened provisions of Act; (g) accounts not kept properly; (h) is branch of unregistered trade union.

[Registration] What is the appeal process for registration refusal?
According to Section 23 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, any person aggrieved by refusal of Registrar to register may within 30 days appeal to the Court. On appeal, Court may make such order as it thinks proper. Any person aggrieved by decision of Court may appeal to Court of Appeal. When trade union appeals and appeal is dismissed, trade union deemed dissolved from date of dismissal.

[Registration] What must be in a trade union constitution?
According to Section 28 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, constitution of every trade union must provide for all matters specified in the Schedule, and shall not be so altered as to cease to contain provision for all such matters. Copy of every new constitution and alteration must be sent to Registrar within one month and registered upon payment of prescribed fee.
