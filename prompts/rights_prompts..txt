RIGHTS AND PROTECTIONS PROMPTS - PART II
[Employee Rights] What are the basic employee rights under Section 4 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005?
According to Section 4 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, every employee shall have the right to: (a) take part in the formation of a trade union or federation of trade union; (b) be a member of a trade union, subject to its constitution. Additionally, every member of a trade union has the right subject to the constitution of that trade union to: (a) take part outside working hours, or with the consent of the employer within working hours, in the lawful activities of a trade union of which he or she is a member; (b) to seek and hold office in a trade union of which he or she is a member, subject to its constitution; (c) to take part in the election of workplace representatives where provision is made for such election; (d) to be elected or appointed and serve as a workplace representative where provision is made for this; (e) to exercise any other right conferred by this Act.

[Employee Rights] What protections exist against discrimination for trade union activities?
According to Section 5 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, no employer, and no person acting on behalf an employer, shall, with respect to any employee or person seeking employment: (a) require that he or she shall not join or shall relinquish membership in a trade union or shall not participate in lawful trade union activities; (b) discriminate or take or threaten any prejudicial action, including disciplinary action or dismissal, against such employee or person by reason of trade union membership or participation in lawful trade union activities, exercise of any right conferred by this Act, or participation in any proceeding under this Act.

[Employer Rights] What are the basic employer rights under the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act?
According to Section 6 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, every employer has the right to: (a) take part in the formation of an employers' organisation; (b) be a member of an organisation, in accordance with its constitution. Every member of an employers' organisation has the right, subject to the constitution of that employers' organisation to: (a) take part in the lawful activities of any such organisation of which is a member; (b) to hold office in any such organisation of which is a member, in accordance with its constitution; (c) to exercise any right conferred by this Act.

[Employee Rights] What protections exist against discrimination in trade union membership?
According to Section 7(4) of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, no union or organisation shall discriminate in its constitution or through its actions against any person on the grounds of race, colour, national extraction, social origin, religion, political opinion, sex, marital status, family responsibilities, age or disability, or impose any condition, restriction or obligation which is discriminatory.

[Employee Rights] What is the right not to join a trade union?
According to Section 7(2) of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, any person who has the right to join has the right not to join such trade union or organisation. Any term in an agreement which purports to require that one of the terms and conditions of employment of an employee shall be that the employee must become a member of a trade union shall be null and void.

[Employee Rights] What remedies are available for infringement of freedom of association?
According to Section 11 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, any complaint of infringement of Part II may be presented to the Labour Court. Where the Court finds that the complaint is well founded, it shall make such order as it deems necessary in the circumstances to ensure compliance with the provisions of this Part, which shall include: (i) reinstatement if it is requested by the employee; (ii) the restoration to him or her of any benefit or advantage; and (iii) the payment of compensation for damages suffered and, where appropriate, of a punitive indemnity.

[Employee Rights] Can an employee be dismissed for participating in trade union activities?
According to Section 5(3) of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, nothing in this section shall be interpreted as preventing an employer from fairly dismissing, or disciplining by means of dismissal, an employee for a valid reason, in accordance with the Labour Act or any other relevant law or regulation. However, dismissal cannot be for trade union activities.

[Employer Rights] What is the protection against trade union interference?
According to Section 8 of the Zanzibar Labour Relations Act 2005, no employer or employers' organisation and no person acting on behalf of an employer or employers' organisation, shall promote the establishment of an employees' organisation under the domination of an employer or employers' organisation, or shall support an employees' trade union by financial or other means with the object of placing such trade union under the control of an employer or employers' organisation.
